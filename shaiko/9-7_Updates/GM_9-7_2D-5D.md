## How to enumerate nodes

For each group **Gj** with generator (the first row below), enumerate all fully symmetric points:

- **G2**: pick each axis $k=1..d$, take points $(\pm \lambda_1)$ on that axis (both signs).  
- **G3/G4/G5**: same with $\lambda_2/\lambda_3/\lambda_p$ on a single axis.
- **G6**: choose unordered pairs of distinct axes $\{i,j\}$, place $(\pm \lambda_1,\pm \lambda_1)$ on those axes (four sign choices).
- **G7**: choose ordered pairs $(i,j)$ with $i\ne j$, place $(\pm \lambda_1,\pm \lambda_2)$ (four sign choices).
- **G8**: choose unordered triples $\{i,j,k\}$, place $(\pm \lambda_1,\pm \lambda_1,\pm \lambda_1)$ (eight sign choices).
- **G9**: take all $2^d$ sign patterns for $(\lambda_0,\ldots,\lambda_0)$.

Each node in a group **uses the *same* group weight** shown above (these are weights for the *sum* over the orbit).

## Notes

- The constants above are those used in **DCUHRE’s** degree‑9/7 family (Fortran routines **D09HRE** and **D07HRE**). They stem from Genz’s *Imbedded Family of Fully Symmetric Numerical Integration Rules* (SIAM J. Numer. Anal., 1983). In DCUHRE, extra axis nodes at $\lambda_p$ are present to support null‑rules (error estimation); their weights are 0 in the basic 9/7 rules.
- Negative weights are expected in high‑degree fully symmetric rules.
- Precision: the family uses fixed decimal constants (e.g., $\lambda_0^2=0.4707$). Using `double` is customary; `long double` may reduce roundoff in extreme anisotropy.
- To map to **[0,1]^d**, transform each node $x\mapsto (x+1)/2$ and scale each weight by $2^{-d}$.
